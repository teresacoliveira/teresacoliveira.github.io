Biography & Core Focus

I am a biochemist and cell biologist with more than two decades of research experience. Over the years, my work has focused on mitochondrial health biomarkers, metabolism, toxicology, and neuroscience, alongside a deep, structural specialization in research data management and metascience.

My current initiatives focus heavily on developing and applying automated tools for screening research rigor and reporting practices, building institutional dashboards for monitoring transparency indicators, and designing workflows that strengthen documentation and methodological clarity across scientific teams.

Career Trajectory & Goals

My scientific career evolved from foundational benchwork in experimental biomedicine to a broader mission of strengthening transparency across the life sciences. Having earned my PhD in Cell Biology from the University of Coimbra, my early years exploring cellular metabolic stress grounded me in strict experimental design.

However, navigating the bench highlighted structural challenges that limit the reliability and reuse of biomedical outputs—particularly gaps in documentation and metadata. This led me to systematically master tools like SQL, Python, R, and Power BI to bridge experimental and computational perspectives.

"Doing science at the bench is demanding work. Beyond what they already do, scientists need practical, uniform processes to document what they do, why, when, and how, so that the information generated can be transparent, repeatable, and fully utilized by society."

My core goal is to raise awareness and increase data literacy among laboratory researchers, translating complex workflows into accessible guidelines. This effort is highlighted in my recent publication in Physiological Reviews, which outlines clear, actionable frameworks for data stewardship in experimental biomedicine.
